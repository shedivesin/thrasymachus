Ancient Greek with Thrasymachus, Chapter VII

Ἕλληνες ἀεὶ παῖδές ἐστε, γέρων δὲ Ἕλλην οὐκ ἔστιν.
You Greeks are always children, there is no Greek old man.
(An Egyptian priest, as quoted by Plato, Timaeus)

Present Middle Participle

You have seen that there are several ways to modify a noun: with an adjective, another noun in apposition, a prepositional phrase, and a relative clause. Be sure you understand the examples below:

ἡ γυνή
the woman
(article + noun)

ἡ αὐτὴ γυνή
the same woman
(noun modified by adjective)

ἡ γυνή, ἡ μήτηρ
the woman, the mother
(noun + noun in apposition)

ἡ γυνὴ ἐν οἰκίᾳ
the woman in the house
(noun + prepositional phrase)

ἡ γυνὴ ἣν βλέπω
the woman whom I see
(noun + relative clause)

Remember hat a noun and the adjective modifying it will agree in case, number, and gender.

In each of the examples above, "the woman" is specified: the same woman, for instance, not the tall or busy woman; the woman, the mother, not the woman, my friend; the woman in the house, not the one in the city; the woman whom I see, not the one who sees me.

There is another way to modify a noun, with a participal or participial phrase.

We use this form of modification in English as well:

ἡ γυνὴ φιλουμένη
the loving woman

ἡ γυνὴ βαινομένη ἐν πόλει
the woman walking in the city

A participal is an adjective formed from a verb. Given its function (as an adjective) and derivation (from a verb) it must be identified in adjectival and verbal terms.

A participle is an adjective, not a main verb that has an ending indicating its person. "The boy running in the street" is not a sentence ("The boy runs [is running] in the street" is a sentence).

βαινομένη is PRESENT, MIDDLE, PARTICIPLE, NOMINATIVE, FEMININE, SINGULAR.

Look at the following examples and observe how the present middle participle is formed:

ὁ ἄνηρ πεμπόμενος τὰ δῶρα

οἳ ἅνδρες πεμπόμενοι τὰ δῶρα

αἱ γυναῖκες παιδευόμεναι τὰ παιδία

τὸ παιδίον φιλούμενον τὸν θεόν

τὰ παιδία φιλούμενα τὸν θεόν

You are now learning the present middle participle. You will learn the active and passive as well.

The translation of the middle participle, just as that of the middle indicative, may be essentially the same in English as the active, but keep in mind the concept of the middle voice.

See Thrasymachus p. 27 for the middle participal forms for contracted verbs.

The present middle participle is formed by taking the present stem (from the first principal part) plus the 1st-2nd declension –ομενος, –η, –ον suffix. Thus πεμπόμενος, –η, –ον.

ὁ ἀνὴρ πεμπόμενος τὰ δῶρα ἐστι ἀγαθός.

οἱ ἄνδρες μαχόμενοι εἰσιν ἰσχυροί.

βλέπω τὰς γυναῖκας παιδευομένας τὰ παιδία.

πέμπω τὰ δῶρα τοῖς παιδίοις μανθανομένοις τὰ μαθήματα.

One way to think about a sentence when you are looking at it carefully is to consider the "bare bones": the subject, verb, and direct object or predicate nominative if relevant. The "bare bones" of the first sentence above are ὁ ἀνήρ ἐστι ἀγαθός. The rest is a participial phrase that modifies the subject. Similarly, the "bare bones" of the third sentence are βλέπω τὰς γυναῖκας.

In English, we tend to use a relative clause instead of a participial phrase: "The philosophers teaching the lessons are wise" and "The philosophers who teach the lessons are wise" mean the same thing.

οἱ φιλόσοφοι διδασκόμενοι τὰ μαθήματά εἰσι σοφοί.

οἱ φιλόσοφοι οἳ διδάσκουσι τὰ μαθήματά εἰσι σοφοί.

Answer Key

    1. πιστευομένων. participle, middle, gen, plu, masc, fem, neut. Trusting. πιστευομένου πιστευομένης πιστευομένου
    2. φαινομένους. participle, middle, accu, plu, masc. Seeming. φαινόμενον
    3. πασχόμενοι. participle, middle, nom, plu, masc. Suffering. πασχόμενος
    4. λειπομένῳ. participle, middle, dat, sing, masc, neut. Leaving. λειπομένοις
    5. λειπομένη. participle, middle, nom, sing, fem. Leaving. λειπόμεναι
    6. κρινομένου. participle, middle, gen, sing, masc, neut. Deciding. κρινομένων
    7. βλεπομένῃ. participle, middle, dat, sing, fem. Looking at. βλεπομένιας
    8. βλεπομέναις. participle, middle, dat, plu, fem. Looking at. βλεπομένῃ
    9. ἀκουόμεναι. participle, middle, nom, plu, fem. Hearing. ἀκουόμενη
    10. ἀκουομένους. participle, middle, accu, plu, masc. Hearing. ἀκουόμενον

    1. The king trusting that man sends the gifts. ὁ βασιλεὺς πιστευόμενος ἐκείνῳ τῷ ἀνδρὶ πέμπει τὰ δῶρα. πιστευόμενος is nom, sing, masc.

    2. The young men are foolish and do not trust the women sending gifts. οἱ νεανίαι εἰσι μωροὶ καὶ οὐ πιστεύουσι ταῖς γυναιξὶ ταῖς πεμπομέναις τὰ δῶρα. πεμπομέναις is dat, plu, fem.

    3. The same gods looking at men rule in heaven. οἱ αὐτοὶ θεοὶ βλεπόμενοι ἀνθρώπους ἀνάσσουσιν ἐν οὐρανῷ. βλεπόμενοι is nom, plu, masc.

    1. The man sending the gift to you which is beautiful is now going into the city. ὁ ἀνὴρ πεμπόμενος ὑμῖν τὸ δῶρον ὁ ἐστι καλὸν νῦν βαίνει εἰς πόλιν. Complex. πεμπόμενος is participle, middle, nom, sing, masc. ὑμῖν is dat, plu. βαίνει is present, active, third person sing.

    2. What does the young man who is in the city suffer? τὶ πάσχει ὁ νεανίας ὅς ἐστίν ἐν πόλει; Complex. τὶ is accu, neut, sing. νεανίας is nom, sing, masc. πόλει is dat, sing, fem.

    3. These lords, fighting in the city which we love, are terrible. οὖτοι οἳ ἄνακτες, μαχόμενοί ἐν τῇ πόλει ἣν φιλοῦμεν, εἰσιν δεινοί. Complex. μαχόμενοί is participle, middle, nom, plu, masc. ἣν is accu, fem, sing. φιλοῦμεν is present, active, first person plu.

    1. The woman teaching the child honors the goddess. ἡ γυνὴ παιδευομένη τὸν παῖδα τιμᾷ τὴν θεάν. ἣ παιδεύει τὸν παῖδα. The woman who teaches the child honors the goddess.

    2. The men themselves, walking in the city, look at the children. αὐτοί οἱ ἄνθρωποι βαινόμενοί ἐν τῇ πόλει βλέπουσι τοὺς παῖδας. οἳ βαίνουσι ἐν τῇ πόλει. The men themselves who walk in the city look at the children.

    3. I see the men teaching the lessons. ἐγὼ ὁρῶ τους ἀνθρώπους παιδευομένους τὰ μαθήματα. οἳ παιδεύουσι τὰ μαθήματα. I see the men who are teaching the children.
